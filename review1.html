It may have taken me over twenty years, but I think I’ve found the best restaurant in the United States. It is a very, very rare occasion that I praise a single restaurant or bakery this highly, but I truly believe Création deserves this acclaim.

The whole layout of Création, first of all, is excellent. On the right sprawls the stupendous restaurant, with an open kitchen so that anyone can look over and enjoy the cooking spectacles. And that, I can promise, is a sight to see. The highly technical and passionate Head Chef Louis Jean-Benoït, the wonderfully extravagant Assistant Chef Pierre de la Rue, and and the meticulously detailed patissiere Rose Anne-Thérèse have truly proven their own in their individual crafts. On the left is a bakery decorated with a modern and yet artistic eye, with blacks, reds, and pale creams chasing each other in elaborate designs. Enough said, you have to actually be there to see how absolutely stunning it is.

The staff was amazing. I love how each and every server put their all into serving with pleasant cordiality. I saw an angry couple treating one of the servers terribly, even threw one of the bowls of soup on the ground, but he responded with absolute politeness. True professionalism right there.

Finally, of course, the food. After all I’ve said with everything else, you would expect at least something would be not amazing. But no-the Mille Fuelle is delightfully layered, Croquembouche is crispy and flaky to the max, and the Éclairs from Création bakery are among the coldest, creamiest, best Éclairs I’ve tasted. All of the dishes in the restaurant itself-whether Feta, Roasted Red Pepper, and Basil spaghetti or french onion soup-are likewise as good. Instead of the gluten-free restriction restricting anything, it instead enhanced the whole experience and proved to be on of the most ingeniously constructed concepts out there. Twelve out of ten.

- Gord On Ramsay
